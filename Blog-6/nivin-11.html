Organizations often explore two main delivery models, namely staff augmentation and project outsourcing, when seeking external assistance to complete significant IT projects. Staff augmentation involves hiring temporary workers to fill short-term job vacancies within your company through outsourcing. Project outsourcing shares some similarities with staff augmentation but also has some key differences. The main distinction between the two models is that with staff augmentation, you are investing in engineering resources while retaining control over your team. Conversely, with outsourcing, you are buying a pre-arranged, specific outcome and sharing the risks with the outsourcing vendor.

Benefits of IT Staff Augmentation

Although staff augmentation and project outsourcing can have advantages in specific situations, the former is typically advantageous for companies with project-based operations that are continually changing. Through IT staff augmentation, companies can engage outsourced expertise as required while maintaining direct supervision over their personnel.

Filling Gaps in the Workforce

One advantage of IT staff augmentation is the flexibility to hire based on specific needs. If you have gaps in your IT workforce that require filling, staff augmentation can be an effective solution for expanding your team. This model enables you to access tech talent from around the world and integrate skilled technical resources into your in-house development team for either short- or long-term periods, depending on your requirements.

Paying For What You Need

Another advantage of IT staff augmentation is that it can help organizations save money in the long run. This model allows you to hire temporary workers, rather than full-time employees, which can be a significant cost savings. With staff augmentation, you only pay for the resources you need for as long as you need them.

Crucial Time Saver

IT staff augmentation can also be a time-saving solution for business leaders. The process of creating job postings, interviewing candidates, and handling paperwork to hire full-time employees can be time-consuming. However, with IT staff augmentation, this process can be eliminated, allowing business leaders to focus on other important tasks.

Maintaining Control Over Existing Staff

IT staff augmentation offers the advantage of retaining control over your existing staff. Outsourcing a project to a third party means entrusting your work to strangers. With staff augmentation, you can opt to work with a temporary team of your choice. You have the liberty to select who leads the project and which areas to prioritize.

Choosing IT staff augmentation instead of project outsourcing can also help you avoid certain geographical challenges. When outsourcing, you may have to rely on a team located in a different region or even a different time zone, which can lead to communication and coordination difficulties. With staff augmentation, your team is based on-site, eliminating these issues and allowing for better collaboration and productivity.

How does it work?

Having the right resources and talent is crucial for the success of any business. However, sometimes an organization may lack the necessary talent to achieve its objectives. This is where IT staff augmentation can be helpful. Let's take a closer look into how it operates.

1. Determining Needs

The first step in the process is to assess your outsourcing needs and goals. This will enable you to build a team of skilled professionals who possess the required expertise, creativity, and energy to complete the project within the stipulated time and budget.

2. Sourcing Talent

The next step is to select a reliable and reputable talent source. It is crucial to carefully evaluate and choose a technical staff firm that thoroughly screens its workers. This ensures that your project is handled by highly qualified and competent professionals.

3. On-Boarding Talent

Once you have assembled your team, it is essential to define clear roles and responsibilities. This will prevent any confusion and ensure that the project stays on course. Encourage your team members to ask questions and provide feedback to foster a collaborative and cohesive environment.

4. Providing Ongoing Support

To ensure that your project meets your standards and deadlines, ongoing support is critical. Simply hiring temporary staff is not enough; you need to provide continuous guidance and supervision to your team. A competent leader can guide the team toward a successful outcome.
