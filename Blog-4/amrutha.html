ERPS: What Is It?

Enterprise Resource Planning systems (ERPS) integrate all core business processes into a single software application. With an ERP accounts receivable module, businesses can manage receivables and cash flow more efficiently. This module can assist you in improving your invoicing process, tracking customer payments, and identifying late payments.

How does ERP automation help improve cash flow?

There are some ways ERP automation can improve your cash flow. When selecting software, prioritize the features that matter most to you.

1. Automated Work Processes

Your staff can better manage its workload with the assistance of automated work processes. It frees them up to focus on more challenging and fulfilling work that calls for human intelligence by removing tedious and time-consuming tasks from their job duties.

2. Better Customer Service

Better service to customers can be provided with an effective accounts receivable procedure. Customer satisfaction and solid relationships can be maintained with prompt and accurate responses to inquiries. The independent invoice management feature will also delight customers.

3. Faster Invoice Process

You can quickly and easily create invoices with the ERP system's accurate and current information. As a result, your turnaround time for accounts receivable will be reduced due to this faster delivery of invoices.

4. Real-Time Data Analytics

Real-time reports can be generated by ERP systems. This information can be used by managers to make decisions quickly. It can also be used as a tool for transparency, allowing employees and the AR team to monitor company performance.

5. Reduced Errors

Errors in the accounts receivable process are reduced by automated ERP systems. There may be fewer disputes over invoices and faster payments if errors are reduced. Additionally, it lowers the likelihood of cash flow shortfalls and missed opportunities.

6. Track Customer Payments

When a customer makes a payment, you'll know right away, and you can look for patterns in payments over time by tracking them. You will be able to make better decisions regarding credit terms, discounts, and pricing with this information.

What are some important indicators of cash flow?

There are numerous accounting KPIs. Using too many can, however, become overwhelming. From these top choices, narrow your choices.

1. Accounts Receivable to Sales Ratio

The percentage of sales that are subject to accounts receivable is determined by this measurement. The better, the lower the number. Fewer sales are held in accounts receivable, freeing up more cash for the business.

2. Cash Conversion Cycle

The number of days it takes for a business to go from cash in to cash out is calculated using this metric. The number of days it takes to sell products, collect receivables, and pay suppliers are all included in the cash conversion cycle. Better is a shorter cycle.

3. Days Sales Outstanding

The average number of days required to collect payments on accounts receivable is measured by DSO. Your AR team's collection performance improves when there are fewer days. Additionally, it suggests a lower likelihood of cash flow issues.

4. Inventory Turnover Ratio

The number of times a company's inventory is sold and replaced in a given time frame is the subject of this metric. The better the company's liquidity position, the higher the number. Additionally, it indicates that the business is making more money from its inventory.
