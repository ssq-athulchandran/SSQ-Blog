There are several ways to integrate Bitcoin payment solutions into your website's payment gateway, and we've compiled the top 5 methods.

1. Stripe:

San Francisco's payment platform, Stripe, offers two ways to integrate Bitcoin payments into websites. It provides a flexible API that enables Bitcoin Checkout with tailored payment forms for desktop and mobile devices. Stripe also offers Checkout in multiple languages such as English, Chinese, French, German, Italian, and Spanish.

2. BitPay:

A major Bitcoin operator, partners with popular brands such as Microsoft, Virgin Galactic, and Newegg. The company offers various payment solutions and add-ons for shopping carts like Magento, Drupal Commerce, Zen Cart, WordPress eCommerce, OpenCart, X-Cart, and more.

BitPay provides three payment plans tailored to different organization sizes and types. There are no transaction fees for up to $1,000 per day or 30 transactions per month and a 1% processing fee for larger amounts. Merchants can send daily or weekly payments in USD or EUR to their bank accounts, depending on their selected plan.

3. Coinbase:

Utilized by big names such as Dell, Wikimedia, and Expedia, provides straightforward checkout processes, payment buttons, and APIs for merchants to develop their own Bitcoin payment solutions Converting BTC to local currency incurs a 1% charge by the company. Coinbase is an excellent option for small businesses or startups as there are no fees for the first $1,000,000.

4. CoinGate:

It provides a range of solutions for businesses, such as APIs, e-commerce plugins, and Point of Sale (PoS) applications for web, Android, and iOS with payment buttons. Companies can accept Bitcoin payments and receive payouts in USD, EUR, or BTC.

5. Cryptopay:

Cryptopay is a UK-based service that facilitates the buying and selling of bitcoins. Its merchant payment gateway enables businesses to accept Bitcoin/Litecoin on their websites without requiring the creation of a digital wallet. Developers can access the company's API and software development kit for Ruby and PHP, NodeJS, and SSL-secured endpoints. Cryptopay levies a 1% processing fee.
