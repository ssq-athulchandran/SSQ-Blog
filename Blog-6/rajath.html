The process of tracking goods throughout the supply chain—from purchase to production to final sale—is referred to as an inventory management system. It is also called as an inventory system. It manages how your business approaches inventory management. Every business has its own way of managing inventory depending on the type and size of the business.

Importance of Inventory Management System

A system to precisely track and control stock is necessary for any business that handles it. You will be having absolutely no idea of the inventory levels without an inventory tracking system, and you'll quickly run into situations where your business has too much or too little inventory. Inventory systems let you know how many parts or ingredients are required to make or put together your finished product.

Without this information, you can either have too much inventory, or you can have too little inventory and not be able to meet customer demand.

There are numerous types of systems that can be adopted, from simple approaches to comprehensive solutions.

Types of Inventory Systems

There are two main methods companies use to manage their inventory: Periodic system and Perpetual system.

Periodic Inventory System: Periodic inventory is a type of inventory management that depends only on the number of items in stock. Businesses that use a periodic system regularly count their stock on a regular basis, say every three to six months, to ensure that stock levels are consistent with sales figures.

Perpetual Inventory System: Perpetual Inventory is a system that tracks inventory as goods are received, produced, sold, or returned to stores. With less reliance on stock taking for accuracy, perpetual inventory systems typically provide the most recent inventory figures.

Managing Inventory Using Spreadsheets

It is a great way to manage your inventory at a basic level using excel. However, it has some disadvantages also. When using spreadsheets, human errors can quickly escalate into costly issues.

Additionally, flexibility and accuracy can frequently be scarce.

  • Merit: Spreadsheets are cheap, genuinely simple to setup, and turn out great for organizations with basic requirements.
  • Demerit: Your inventory management system also suffers damage if the spreadsheet fails. Additionally, your business will quickly become cumbersome as it becomes more complex.

Basic Inventory Management Software:

Basic inventory management features are included in many cloud-based applications. Although it is not as comprehensive as dedicated software, it is for many the natural next step after spreadsheets. However, you might discover that you still require spreadsheets to meet your particular needs.

  • Merit: The majority of applications are cloud-based, so there is no need to locate and install a new solution.
  • Demerit: Usually difficult to match some specific needs.

Enterprise Resource Planning System:

Instead of purchasing multiple cloud components that are interconnected, you purchase a single ERP solution to cover all aspects of business planning. Typically, this entails selecting and putting in the "modules" you require, including inventory management.

  • Merit: A single system that can handle inventory, accounting, supply chain, human resources, and more.
  • Demerit: Expensive (with ongoing costs for maintenance and upgrades), hard to implement, and takes a lot of time and effort to set up; custom software might be better for every job.

Warehouse Management System:

While inventory management systems should not be confused with warehouse management systems, they can be extremely useful for inventory control. While many inventory systems may have some functionality to assist with this, it will not be as comprehensive as a specialized warehousing tool. Warehouse management is all about giving your warehouse team the tools they need to operate as efficiently as possible.

Dedicated Inventory Management System

Software designed specifically for inventory management enables you to track and manage stock. You will be able to access your data anywhere, at any time, and sync the software with your other cloud applications if it is cloud-based.

  • Merit: An effective solution that is simple to incorporate into other systems. Completely adaptable and made just for the specific task.
  • Demerit: Needs to be setup right for it to work well.
