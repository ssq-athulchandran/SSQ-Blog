4. Electronic Data Interchange (Edi) And Chargeback Management Tools

The use of EDI streamlines and digitizes business partner communications. It is an electronic document interchange that takes the place of mail, faxes, and emails. It is the place where you and your business partners discuss important information about orders and shipping. It is now considered standard documentation. It serves as proof to retailers that they are receiving the right product at the appropriate time.
Essentially, EDI comes in two flavors: vendor-managed and self-managed.
Self-managed EDI is rather complex, but if you have the resources, procedures, and personnel to quickly onboard new clients, develop new maps, and keep up with needed changes, you might be successful in operating your own EDI services. There is, however, a choice if you choose to have a vendor organize and manage everything for you.
In vendor-managed EDI, the vendor is in charge of every aspect of EDI, including testing and transaction monitoring, building and managing maps, providing technical support, scaling up or down, and managing communication networks. Your vendor needs to be able to handle anything. For some consumer products companies, utilizing built-in, controlled EDI is the more effective and efficient choice. You know your business; it all comes down to what you need. Neither choice is better or worse than the other. In either case, we're here to support you.
