In staff augmentation, workers are outsourced to meet an organization's current staffing needs. For instance, if a company intends to create a gaming app and needs seven developers, but their in-house development team only has four, staff augmentation comes into play. Hiring four skilled developers with sound expertise in building robust apps could take a month or two, delaying the project launch and depriving the company of predicted revenue.

By utilizing IT staff augmentation, the company can quickly hire the three additional developers within weeks, speeding up the project's completion and product launch. This model is typically cooperative and involves outsourcing, enabling the company to avoid the legal and paperwork hassles of full-time employment once the app hits the market. Furthermore, the developers who worked on the project can continue working for the company, eliminating the need for firing them.

Types of IT Staff Augmentation

There are three ways to implement staff augmentation:

1. Commodity-based augmentation:

This type of augmentation involves hiring additional staff who may not possess highly specialized skills, but can handle basic tasks required by the business. Commodity-based augmentation is useful when there is a shortage of employees and businesses need to hire people quickly, or when they need to fill positions that do not require high levels of skill.

2. Skill-based augmentation:

This staff augmentation involves hiring workers with specialized skills, higher levels of education, and experience than average workers. Skilled workers can complete tasks more efficiently than others in the company and bring value to the organization with their unique skills.

3. Highly-skilled augmentation:

This type of augmentation involves hiring highly skilled individuals from outside organizations for specific projects that require expertise, such as developing new products or solving complex problems. Highly-skilled augmentation can be classified into two categories: short-term and long-term.

Short-term staff augmentation is for temporary employees who are hired to assist with a specific task, such as an intern who can help with design elements during an ad campaign. In contrast, long-term staff augmentation involves hiring someone for a year or more. This could be because the employee's skills have become indispensable to the business and they are needed for the long haul.

When to Use Staff Augmentation?

Determining the appropriate circumstances to increase headcount is crucial for businesses. Here are five scenarios where staff augmentation can be beneficial:

1. Reinforcing in-house teams

Even if a company has in-house software developers, it may still be necessary to supplement or enhance current expertise while launching a new product or starting a new project. In such cases, augmenting the IT team can be a good idea.

2. Bridging skill gaps

Multiple projects may require different skills in various specialties, and the demand for these skills is constantly changing. Staff augmentation can help by adding experienced developers who possess specialized skills, thereby eliminating the need to train in-house employees.

3. Expanding teams quickly

If a company needs a local developer with deep knowledge of specific technologies, the process of finding and hiring an expert can be time-consuming. Staff augmentation can save time by allowing companies to contact vendors, review developer resumes, and interview them.

4. Meeting deadlines

Hiring can be a lengthy process that takes an average of two months, from job posting to onboarding. Staff augmentation is an effective way to increase IT staff and meet needs regardless of the contract length. Companies can also augment from around the world with instant access to a global pool of software developers.

5. Other models are not feasible

When a company needs more control over the developer's work and wants to manage their workload itself, other models may not be suitable. Smoothly integrating software developers into the team can also be an additional motivation to focus on the business.
